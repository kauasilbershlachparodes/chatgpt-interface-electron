Suggest trendy recipes for a quick weeknight dinner

ChatGPT said:

Here are some trendy, quick weeknight dinner ideas that feel modern, flavorful, and don’t take forever:

🥢 Chili Crisp Honey Garlic Noodles

A huge trend thanks to the popularity of chili crisp.

  • Toss noodles with garlic, soy sauce, honey, and chili crisp

  • Add sautéed mushrooms or shrimp

  • Finish with green onions + sesame seeds
    ⏱️ ~20 min

🌮 Smash Burger Tacos

A viral mashup inspired by smash burgers.

  • Press seasoned ground beef directly onto tortillas

  • Cook beef-side down in a hot pan

  • Flip, add cheese, and crisp the tortilla

  • Top with lettuce, pickles, and burger sauce
    ⏱️ ~15–20 min

🍚 Salmon Rice Bowl (Emily Mariko–style)

Made famous on TikTok.

  • Reheat rice with an ice cube trick (steams it!)

  • Add flaked salmon, avocado, soy sauce, and sriracha mayo

  • Optional: roasted seaweed snacks
    ⏱️ ~20 min

🥗 Green Goddess Chickpea Salad Wraps

A plant-based spin on the viral Green Goddess salad.

  • Mash chickpeas with herbs, yogurt or tahini, lemon, garlic

  • Add crunchy veggies (cucumber, cabbage)

  • Wrap in tortillas or lettuce cups
    ⏱️ ~15 min

🍝 Creamy Boursin Tomato Pasta

A baked pasta trend using Boursin cheese.

  • Roast cherry tomatoes, garlic, and Boursin

  • Mix with hot pasta and pasta water

  • Add spinach or grilled chicken
    ⏱️ ~25 min

🌯 Buffalo Chicken Flatbread Wraps

A quick comfort-food upgrade.

  • Toss shredded rotisserie chicken in buffalo sauce

  • Layer on flatbread with ranch or blue cheese

  • Add lettuce + carrots, fold and toast
    ⏱️ ~15 min

🍳 Kimchi Fried Rice with Jammy Eggs

A Korean-inspired staple featuring kimchi.

  • Stir-fry rice with kimchi, garlic, and gochujang

  • Top with a soft-boiled (jammy) egg

  • Finish with sesame oil + scallions
    ⏱️ ~20 min
